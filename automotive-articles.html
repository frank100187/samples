Car Overheating: Main Reasons And How to Prevent

Car overheating is a situation no vehicle owner wants to face. What may seem like a minor issue can quickly escalate into severe damage to both the vehicle and potentially compromise the safety of the driver. So, why does a car overheat? The primary cause is usually a malfunction within the vehicle's cooling system. When the cooling system fails to regulate the engine’s temperature properly, overheating becomes inevitable, leading to costly repairs and safety hazards if not addressed promptly.

How is COVID-19 Affecting the Automotive Industry?

Since the Coronavirus outbreak in December 2019, the auto industry has made efforts to show unwavering resilience. Despite these efforts, the outbreak continues to cause widespread global concern and economic hardship for communities, consumers, and businesses.

This has had a massive impact on automotive sales and services since consumers are skeptical of purchasing new vehicles amidst the uncertainties of the pandemic. As such, manufacturers have pivoted to offering online services and contactless delivery to adapt to the changing landscape.
